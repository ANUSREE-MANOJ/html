A plotter is a specialized output device commonly used in the field of computer-aided design (CAD) and graphic design to produce high-quality, large-scale prints of technical drawings, maps, or detailed illustrations. Unlike standard printers, plotters use pens, pencils, or other drawing instruments to create precise lines on paper or other media. Plotters are particularly advantageous when intricate and accurate representations are crucial, such as in architectural and engineering designs. There are two main types of plotters: pen plotters and electrostatic plotters. Pen plotters employ a series of pens with varying colors and line thicknesses, and they move across the paper to create detailed and colorful plots. Electrostatic plotters, on the other hand, use electrostatic charges and toner to produce images on a special kind of paper. Plotter technology has evolved over the years, and while they were once a common tool for design professionals, advances in printer technology have led to a decline in the use of traditional plotters. However, they still find application in specific industries where precision and detail are paramount. The legacy of plotters persists in the term "plot" when referring to generating large-format prints or drawings.
